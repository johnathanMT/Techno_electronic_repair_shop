Tips & Troubleshooting

Repair Guide

Simple articles and DIY tips from our technicians. Try these first — if it still needs a pro, we're one click away.

TV Basics

TV Turns On But No Picture? 5 Checks

Before you panic, check the input source, cables, backlight and standby light. Here's the quick order to test them.

  • Cycle the input/source button
  • Re-seat the HDMI and power cables
  • Shine a torch on the screen (backlight test)
Smart / Android TV

Apps Freezing or Wi-Fi Dropping?

Most smart-TV glitches are fixed by a proper restart and a cache clear, not a repair.

  • Unplug the TV for 60 seconds (cold reboot)
  • Forget and rejoin your Wi-Fi network
  • Clear the app's cache in Settings
Monitors

Monitor Says "No Signal"

Nine times out of ten this is a cable or input issue, not a dead panel.

  • Swap the HDMI/DisplayPort cable
  • Match the monitor input to the port used
  • Test the monitor on another computer
Screen Faults

Lines or Flicker on the Screen

Vertical lines usually point to a panel or T-CON board issue — this one is best left to a technician.

  • Note if lines change when you tap the frame
  • Take a photo of the pattern for us
  • Avoid pressing on the panel
Care & Maintenance

5 Habits That Make a TV Last Longer

Small routines that prevent big, expensive repairs down the line.

  • Use a surge protector
  • Keep vents dust-free and ventilated
  • Lower brightness to reduce heat
Quick Fixes

Remote Not Working?

A dead remote is rarely the TV. Rule out the easy stuff in two minutes.

  • Replace the batteries (check polarity)
  • Check the IR light with a phone camera
  • Clean the button contacts
